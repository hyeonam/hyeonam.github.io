Becoming Uncomfortable

Rhizome 2010 Commissions Proposal

Project title
Becoming uncomfortable

Project Core Concept
Becoming uncomfortable is a series of thirty three relayed videos (10 days/participants including three-to-five days for camera shipping) conveying a social message against conformity. Having lived in two countries (Korea and America), I struggle to adjust to each different space and culture emotionally and physically. Each situation summons different roles, customs, and habits, which bring upon different mental challenges. Through this experience, I have learned different cultures and customs and try to take open mind to others.

In Becoming uncomfortable I ask people to walk backward like I performed previously in Time square, Manhattan. I ask them to try to be comfortable becoming uncomfortable and open their mind to others who have different backgrounds.

I assume this experience could give a chance to understand others to people even they have not had immigrant experience like me. At least they try to think their surrounding in a different way and take flexible societal standard.

Example of Procedure
1) I will send a video camera with 33 SD cards to my friend, "Inmi" (for example) in Pennsylvania through July 1-10.
2) I will ask her to walk backward on a busy street in the Philadelphia downtown.
3) I pay $50 to her and her cameraman.
4) After they have filmed the video, then they need to send SD card including their film to me.
5) They send the video camera with 32 SD card to one of her friends, "David" (for example) in Australia in July 11-20.
6) After Inmi has sent the video camera and 32 SD cards with insurance, they let me know the tracking number of them.
7) After he and his cameraman have filmed his backward walk, they continue this relay.
8) In the meanwhile, when I digitize the video file from SD card, I reverse the footage to show their forward walking from backward walking.
9) Participants continue this relay until the 33th participant will have done this routine completely.
10) Since even these are 33 series of videos, time/space limits the number of experience of participants. That is the reason why I design a website for videos; more people could access to learning and consideration.

Project Background
Space takes on multiple definitions. For me, I understand space as the sum of cultural and social forces that act on me. When I moved from Korea to America, my body became a gauge that felt my displacement and recognized not only the conformity inflicted on me in America, but it also allowed me to deconstruct the rule from my hometown that I had taken for granted. In Becoming Uncomfortable I spread out the feeling of displacement by acting of walking, which is everyday task. Participants perform forward walking and other people seem to be walking backward. However, They were walking backward in the real scene and I simply reversed the footage. The space of being neither here following the correct rule nor there following an incorrect rule is what I try to convey in these series. Because to me, art is not merely meant to be decorative; instead, art can be a question, proposal or solution.
